Magnitude 6+ Earthquake in Year 2015--2020

INTRODUCTION:

As Wikipedia says :"An earthquake (also known as a quake, tremor or temblor) is the shaking of the surface of the Earth resulting from a sudden release of energy in the Earth's lithosphere that creates seismic waves.‚Äù People will not feel earthquakes with smaller magnitudes, but earthquakes of magnitude 6 and above often cause casualties and even cause other natural disasters. Since the technological level of today and for a long time in the future cannot predict the arrival of an earthquake, the so-called prediction is just a coincidence, so it is necessary to continue to observe the occurrence of larger earthquakes.According to the data of the United States Geological Survey (USGS), 805 earthquakes of magnitude 6 and above were recorded between 2015 and 2020.

The Number of Earthquakes by Year:

The number of earthquakes in 17 years was the least, but as the US Geological Survey said: "A short-term increase in earthquakes or a phenomenon of normal earthquake frequency. It seems that there are none."

Magnitude vs Depth of the Earthquakes:

According to USGs,"earthquakes can occur anywhere between the Earth's surface and about 700 kilometres below the surface. For scientific purposes, this earthquake depth range of0-700 km is divided into three zones: shallow, intermediate, and deep".Half of the earthquakes 6+ were shallow(around 32 km depth),and their magnitude were 6,5 and less on the Richter scale.
